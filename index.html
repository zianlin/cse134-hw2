Selected Projects

  1. Bandmates

    Bringing together musicians, bands, and artists.

    Bandmates is an app that brings together musicians with similar taste in music and allows them to connect and chat with each other. I decided to create Bandmates after seeing flyers around campus with students advertising their favorite artists, what instruments they could play, and who they needed in their band. I thought students using paper flyers seemed awfully outdated for 2022, and I decided to get to work on a better way to connect musicians. Everything you see in the app was created by me: the frontend, the backend, the UI, even the logo. Bandmates is slated to launch at five college campuses in early 2023.

  2. Labyrinth

    Navigate the world with your phone in your pocket.

    Labyrinth allows you to navigate to a location through haptic feedback alone. The app calls the Google Maps API with the user's desired destination and method of transport, and it translates it into a series of coded vibrations that buzz whenever the user needs to make a turn. It makes navigation far more accessible to those who are hard of hearing and cannot afford to constantly check their phone while moving. Labyrinth ended up winning the 'Most Technical' award at IEEE's winter 2022 showcase for it's complexity.

  3. Birds of a Feather

    A social network for the classroom.

    Birds of a Feather is a social networking app that connects students with other nearby students who they've shared classes with in the past. Returning to on-campus life for the first time after quarantine, we felt the need to create something that connected people. This app was developed with extensive Agile processes in a real-world environment, with me managing the project and programming the database logic, login system, and user interface.
